Donate
​Every donation counts.

​Consider donating in order to make a substantial impact on child marriage in your community, and in the world. ​Donating is usually the better option if one does not have the time or capability to volunteer in a required setting.


Do not feel limited by the organizations listed below—there are hundreds more that operate on donations.
​1. Girls Not Brides: An over-arching coalition of more than 700 civil society organizations from over 90 nations. They bring child marriage to global attention, build an understanding of what it will take to end child marriage and call for the laws, policies and programs that will make a difference in the lives of millions of girls. 
2. The International Center for Research on Women: Someone needs to keep track. The ICRW does just that. After assessing various holistic approaches to advocate and work against child marriage, the ICRW collects information on approaches that have worked in the past, and the impact, upon which it creates a path of action.
3. Women's Worldwide Web: A crowdsourcing platform to promote girls' and women's economic empowerment worldwide. This often includes donation campaigns against child marriage, in favor of vocational training or education that can open up opportunities for girls in various locations.
4. Forum for African Women Educationalists: An Africa-wide NGO that works in 33 African nations against child marriage. Their work includes shaping governmental policy, advocating against child marriage, and creates different educational models for girls which it then encourages institutions and governments to adopt. 

Visit the PixelProject's Child Marriage page to see various other organizations that work to stop child marriage in different regions of the world.
